vool — figma prototype

VOOL is a mobile app concept for logging and revisiting concerts you’ve attended. To the left is a playthrough of the prototype using my own concert photos. The idea stems from my frustration with trying to find specific shows in a camera roll where everything visually blurs together. genre.exe — Video Playthrough

Contemporary streaming platforms have effectively taken over genre classification, determining how music is labeled, grouped, and understood at scale. These classifications are not just a means for organization; they shape listener perception, influence recommendation systems, and solidify how songs and artists are culturally framed over time.

genre.exe is a web-based genre exploration tool using iTunes, Last.Fm, and Wikipedia APIs. Searching a song returns community-generated tags from Last.fm rather than platform-assigned genres, shifting classification away from corporate systems and toward listener interpretation. Each tag links to its corresponding Wikipedia category, and if a tag doesn't have a wikipedia page, I included a note talking about how music creation is evolving past such rigid categorization.
